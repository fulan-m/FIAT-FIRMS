# FIAT FIRMS

# Fire Incident / Anomalous Temperature events (FIATs) analysis with the Fire Information for Resource Management System (FIRMS) dataset

</div>

# About

<div align="justify">

This repository contains the collections of results obtained during a Research Initiation grant given by the São Paulo Research Foundation (FAPESP) during the period of 08/2025 to 07/2026. This project was contucted under the idea of analyzing the patterns and behaviors of wildfire occurences in the Cerrado biome. Through the use of datasets such as the FIRMS product, fire occurences from November 2000 could be assessed, and cross-analyzed with other meaningful collections, such as vegetation indices (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index - NDVI; Enhanced Vegetation Index - EVI; Leaf Area Index - LAI; Fraction of Photosynthetically Active Radiation FPAR), Green House Gasses emissions (Carbon monoxide - CO; Methane - CH<sub>4</sub>; Nitrogen dioxide - NO<sub>2</sub>; Formaldehyde - CH<sub>2</sub>O) and aerosols.

## **Results from the projects can be found here:**

### [🔥 Wildfire hotspot analysis 🎯](https://github.com/fulan-m/FIAT-FIRMS/tree/main/CIC-UFSCar-2025)

<div align='justify'>

Using fire layers from the NASA | LANCE | FIRMS product, hotpots calculated with Kernel Density Estimates were made over the Cerrado biome in São Paulo state and analyzed and presented as a poster at the "_Congresso de Iniciação Científica – CIC, o Congresso de Iniciação em Desenvolvimento Tecnológico e Inovação – CIDTI e o Congresso de Iniciação Científica do Ensino Médio - CIC-EM_" at the Federal University of São Carlos (UFSCar) in São Carlos, São Paulo, Brazil.

</div>

### [🚨 C.O.M.B.U.S.T.I.O.N.: _Wildfire prection using Random Forest_ 🧯](https://github.com/fulan-m/FIAT-FIRMS/tree/main/C.O.M.B.U.S.T.I.O.N.)

<div align='justify'>

Uses NASA | LANCE | FIRMS fire layers in Google Earth Engine and combines them with environmental variables measured by other products to train a binary model with fire probability estimates. **TO BE** presented at the World Symposium on Artificial Intelligence for Climate Change Adaptation and Mitigation (WS-AI4CCAM) at the _Fluminense_ Federal University (UFF) in Niterói, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.
